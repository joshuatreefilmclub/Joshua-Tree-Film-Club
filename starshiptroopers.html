Review No. 4: Starship Troopers (1997) - Science Fiction/Action

Alien Bugs are coming for Earth! - Watched on 8/29/2025

Directed by:Paul Verhoeven
Starring: Casper Van Dien, Dina Meyer, Neil Patrick Harris

Joshua Tree’s Film Club Rating: ★ ★ ★ ★ ★ (5 out of 5 Stars)

The Verdict:

Today I watched a movie called Starship Troopers a Sci-Fi film involving Kaiju-like bugs who want to invade Earth for its natural resources. This movie was on my watchlist for a long time and I was happy to finally get around to watching it.

This movie was suprisingly good. As I hadn't heard a lot of it, I was expecting kind of like a low-budget cringe alien invasion, but instead I got a film that I would consider a classic, and I don't understand why it isn't talked a lot. The costumes and the set pieces in the movie are really good, and even though the alien bugs don't seem as realistic as we can make them now, I can feel that this movie had a lot of hard work put into it to make it the best that they could. The actors are great and the make the story believable and the dialogues flows without it being like for example a cringe alien invasion movie. My only commentary is that this movie is rated R for really stupid reasons, that don't really bring much to the film. I did some research and I heard that the director is know for making mainly R rated films. But, honestly this film could've easily been a successful IP like Star Wars, but was most likely not due to its R-rated.

While watching this I was also thinking about a video game that I played a little bit with some friends once called Helldivers, which is really popular in the gaming world. Its a video game with the exact same bugs and the type of game-play you would expect if this movie was tranlated into a video game. Than I proceeded to do some research online and figured out that Helldiver is strongly inspired by Starship Troopers. I mean think about it Helldivers is one of the most popular video games of the 2020s, but Starship Troopers isn't popular? There's clearly a problem. As someone who also likes everything in the aspect of business, there is some marketing and IP failures that acould have make Starship Troopers a massive franchise. But maybe its better as a stand-off movie? Well, I don't know.
