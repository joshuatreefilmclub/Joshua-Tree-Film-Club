Review No. 9: Dracula (1931) - Horror/Classic

Universal monsters are long - Watched on 10/11/2025

Directed by:Tod Browning
Starring: Bela Lugosi, Helen Chandler

Joshua Tree’s Film Club Rating: ★ ★ (2 out of 5 Stars)

The Verdict:

Halloween season has started, and I wanted to start watching some Universal Monster films, and eventually finishing them all. I had previously seen the Invisible Man (which I actually enjoyed a lot), and now for my second one I would start with one of the older installments, Dracula from 1931. It may seem shocking that I gave this movie such a low rating (Letterboxd average was 3.6), but in all honesty it wasn't that great.

Don't get me wrong the images look stunning for the time that it was made, but the pacing is dull and boring, and the absence of music for most of the film makes it very long. I really like the actors and I think that it is very well done (a lot of very old movies, have very dramatic and 'fake' acting), especially Bela Lugosi, who plays Dracula himself those an excellent job of portraying the mysterious air of the character. But really what made it almost unbearable to watch was the fact that the pacing was so long and my attention was slowly slipping away. If there was movie and better cutting (which improves by the years!) this movie would have been splendid. Though I still have to give respects to the founding fathers of film as we know it now, and that yeah not everything was perfectly done in the beginning, but wow crazy to say that we went from Dracula 1931 to Nosferatu 2025 (huge improvements).
