Review No. 1: Groundhog Day (1993) - Fantasy/Comedy

A Repeated Comedy - Watched on 8/6/2025

Directed by: Harold Ramis
Starring: Bill Murray, Andie MacDowell

Joshua Tree’s Film Club Rating: ★★★★☆ (4 out of 5 Stars)

The Verdict (SPOILER WARNING):

Groundhog Day (1993) is an amazing comedy movie. I've heard about this movie for a long time, but it seemed to bore me the idea of reliving the same day over and over again. Finally, I pulled the string and decided to watch this film. I can say for a fact, I don't regret doing so. Groundhog Day it is a film about time, routine, self-reflection, and what it means to grow as a person. Set in the small town of Punxsutawney, Pennsylvania, the film traps its main character played by Bill Murray in a single repeating day, and uses that to tell a surprisingly emotional story about change.

He starts out as a sarcastic, bitter weatherman, but as the day repeats endlessly, we get to watch him slowly change, then rebuild himself. The writing is great, the comedic timing is near-perfection, and the pacing of the transformation feels real. The repetition never gets boring thanks to clever script and fresh variations of the same scenes.

But what really makes Groundhog Day a great film and not just a flat comedy movie, is that it blends philosophy and humor so effortlessly. It's rare to find a movie that makes you laugh, then genuinely reflect on your own life, makes you think, and laugh again

The romance, while sweet by the end, feels a little underdeveloped and fake, especially considering how one-sided the time loop experience is (as Bill Murray's the only one who knows it). Still, these are minor issues in a brilliant film.

There isn't much I could say bad about this film, the acting was great from Bill Murray and Andie MacDowell. The story feels kind of slow at some points, but overall the story flows from one end to another smoothly. This is a film that I feel like I could watch multiples times without getting bored. That’s a hard thing to pull off when your entire movie is the same day on repeat. Groundhog Day, deserves its place as a classic film.
