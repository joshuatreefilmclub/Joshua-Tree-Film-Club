Review No. 5: Loving Vincent (2017) - Art/Emotional

Vincent Why? - Watched on 8/31/2025

Directed by:Hugh Welchman, DK Welchman
Starring: Douglas Booth, Robert Gulaczyk, Eleanor Tomlinson

Joshua Tree’s Film Club Rating: ★ ★ ★ ★ ★ (5 out of 5 Stars)

The Verdict:

Today I watched a movie called Loving Vincent. And, wow. I was not ready for this. This movie is about the events leading up to the death and the consequences of the death of Vincent Van Gogh (yes the painter Van Gogh). But here's where it gets even more interesting. The movie was entirely filmed and then each framer where individually painted by-hand. And wow it is so beautifully well done, and it makes you appreciate the art of creativiting and film even more.

Though the the film is beautifully made there are a couple of flaws. The pacing is very dull and I know its meant to appreciate the art more, but it can get really boring at some points. It also took me a little while to figure out the story and I feel like the start was weak compared to the end. I think the creative process and idea behind the movie is really what made it so that I gave it a five stars. If not for the beautiful visuals the film probably wouldn't be very good and overlooked.
